Why it matters

Science is best learned by doing

Under-resourced schools rarely have access to expensive lab equipment or cadaver specimens. ModAnatomy fills that gap with a $30, fully 3D-printable model that puts real physics in students' hands — letting them feel the difference between systole and diastole with every pump.

  • 🤲

    Kinesthetic Learning

    Students physically pump pistons to simulate each cardiac phase, creating genuine muscle memory of how the heart works.

  • 👁️

    Visual Feedback

    Colored water flows through transparent tubing — students see directional flow, valves in action, and chamber fills in real time.

  • 🔧

    Mechanically Transparent

    No motors, no black boxes. Students can see exactly how every part connects — and understand why.

The Problem

A $30 solution to a real gap

Commercial heart models cost hundreds of dollars. Cadaver labs are inaccessible to most public schools. Biology teachers are left with flat diagrams and abstract descriptions — and students are left without the visceral experience of understanding how life works.

ModAnatomy was born from a simple question: what if a student could actually feel the vacuum of diastole and the pressure of systole with their own hands, for the cost of a few school lunches?

Our Approach

Open-source. Low-cost. Built for the classroom.

Every component is 3D-printable on a consumer printer. Assembly uses craft foam, silicone caulk, and aquarium tubing. Files are freely available. Teachers can build it. Students can build it. Anyone can replicate it.

01

Accessibility First

Designed specifically for under-resourced middle and high schools. Every design decision is made with cost and replicability in mind.

02

The Vision

One model. A whole body.

ModAnatomy isn't just a heart model — it's the first piece of a modular organ system. We envision a future where any science classroom can build and interact with mechanical analogs of every major body system.

Bellows for lungs. Filtration columns for kidneys. Peristaltic tubes for digestion. Comparative anatomy kits — fish hearts, amphibian hearts, mammal hearts — all at the same $30 price point.

Prototype V1 — Functional

The Piston Heart Model

A manually-actuated mechanical model that simulates the full cardiac cycle using square pistons, foam-silicone gaskets, and one-way check valves. Fully 3D-printable. Tested for 500+ pump cycles.

Chambers

6 total — 4 heart chambers (left/right atria & ventricles) + 2 reservoir boxes for fluid circulation

Piston Design

20mm × 20mm square cross-section with foam-silicone composite gaskets for watertight vacuum seal

Valves

Aquarium-style external check valves ensure strict unidirectional flow, mimicking the heart's own valve function

Visibility

Transparent 6mm ID silicone tubing with colored water + glycerin for vivid, easily tracked flow visualization

Mechanics

How it works

01

Pull Stroke — Diastole

Students pull the lever arm upward, retracting the piston and creating a vacuum inside the chamber. This suction draws colored water through the inlet check valve — exactly mimicking how the heart's chambers fill with blood during diastole.

02

Push Stroke — Systole

Pressing the lever arm down drives the piston forward, building pressure inside the chamber. The outlet check valve opens and water is ejected into the tubing — simulating the heart's powerful systolic contraction.

03

Valve Action — Unidirectional Flow

One-way check valves prevent backflow in both directions, exactly as the heart's mitral, aortic, tricuspid, and pulmonary valves do. Students can observe that the colored water only ever moves in one direction through the system.

04

The Key Breakthrough — Square Piston Sealing

Creating a vacuum with a square piston is harder than it sounds. Our solution: craft foam cut to the piston face, attached at edges only (center must flex), coated with 100% silicone caulk and cured for 6–24 hours. The result is a compressible, waterproof gasket that seals under negative pressure without requiring circular geometry or O-rings.
