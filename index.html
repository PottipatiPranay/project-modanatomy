The idea in
one sentence
Commercial anatomy models cost hundreds of dollars and sit unused in storage rooms. We're building a suite of mechanical organ model sets — starting with the heart — and renting them directly to schools on a flexible basis, so the cost of access becomes a fraction of the cost of ownership.
Design philosophy

Built to be understood, not just observed.

Every design decision starts with one question: can a student figure out how this works just by looking at and using it? No motors, no hidden mechanisms, no black boxes.

We're targeting schools that don't have access to expensive lab equipment. By renting our models as sets rather than selling them outright, we keep the cost of access low and take on the responsibility of maintenance and updates.

Hands-on by design

Students physically operate each model rather than passively observing. The heart model uses manual pistons — you feel the resistance, the suction, the pressure.

Visually transparent

Colored water flows through clear tubing. One-way valves are visible. Nothing is hidden. The goal is that the mechanics teach the biology.

Radically affordable

By renting model sets rather than selling them, schools pay a fraction of what commercial anatomy tools cost — and never worry about replacement parts or upkeep.

Built for the classroom

Designed specifically for under-resourced middle and high schools. Our rental model means schools get access without the burden of ownership.

Starting with the heart.
Building toward the whole body.

The heart model is our first proof of concept — but Project ModAnatomy's vision is a full rental library of organ system model sets. Each one designed around the same principles: mechanical, transparent, affordable.
